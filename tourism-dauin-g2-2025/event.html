Festival in Dauin

Kinaiyahan Festival

The Kinaiyahan Festival is a vibrant eco-cultural celebration in Dauin held annually in early September, coinciding with the town’s fiesta in honor of San Nicholas de Tolentino on September 10.

What Happens in the Festival

Street Dancing & Showdown Competition — The highlight of the festival is a street dance parade and a showdown where contingents from barangays and schools perform elaborate choreography wearing nature-inspired costumes that celebrate the environment, biodiversity, and Dauin’s natural heritage.

Tribute to Nature, Environment & Heritage — “Kinaiyahan” means “nature” in Cebuano, and the festival centers on honoring the town’s natural resources, marine and land environments, and its commitment to conservation.

Community Involvement — Local barangays, schools, and community groups participate enthusiastically, designing costumes, floats, and performances that depict themes related to nature, biodiversity, conservation, and local identity.

Festive Activities & Side Events — The festival is part of the broader town fiesta weeks. Activities around it include pageants, cultural presentations, fairs, and other community events leading up to September 10.
