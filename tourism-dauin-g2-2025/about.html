About Dauin

Dauin, Negros Oriental is a vibrant coastal municipality celebrated for its pristine shores, rich culture, and lush natural landscapes. Known as the “Gateway to Apo Island,” Dauin has become a world-renowned destination for diving and snorkeling, offering crystal-clear waters, colorful coral reefs, and diverse marine species that attract visitors from all over the world. Its numerous marine sanctuaries are carefully protected, showcasing the community’s dedication to preserving the environment. Beyond the sea, Dauin offers a peaceful rural charm with its hot and cold springs, scenic mountain views, and friendly locals who proudly uphold their traditions and festivals. Whether you're seeking adventure beneath the waves, relaxation along quiet beaches, or a deeper look into local culture, Dauin stands out as one of the most beautiful and enriching destinations in Negros Oriental.

In recent years, Dauin has also grown as a hub for eco-tourism, drawing travelers who appreciate sustainable adventures and authentic local experiences. Resorts and homestays in the area offer a balance of comfort and nature, giving visitors a chance to enjoy peaceful coastal living. The town’s markets, food spots, and community events showcase the flavors and creativity of Dauin’s people, from fresh seafood dishes to handcrafted goods. Despite its growth, Dauin maintains a serene, small-town atmosphere where the rhythm of daily life remains connected to the sea and the land. This blend of natural beauty, culture, and warm hospitality makes Dauin not just a place to visit, but a place to remember.
